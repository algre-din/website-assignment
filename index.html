Some implications of Euthanasia

An implication is a conclusion that can be drawn from something although it is not explicitly stated.
This page would cover the implications Euthanasia has on the political sphere of our world today, as well as its meaning to me personally.

Euthanasia is the action of ending another person’s life because it has been considered that they would be better dead. Another term we learned was assisted dying, which is a form of euthanasia, except the final act is made by the patient themselves. Losing a loved one hurts bad, and it’s harder to watch them suffer in pain. But we have been shown from the bible euthanasia is wrong, because it goes against the principle of the fifth commandment, thou shalt not kill. The bible also has principles such as Christian love that tells us how to respond to the dying. When all we can see is a majority of people, or our own feelings contradicting our beliefs, is when the principles God gave to us would be hard to follow. But in these times, it’s important to hold on to the promises God gave to those who will follow his word.
